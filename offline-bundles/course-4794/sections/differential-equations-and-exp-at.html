Differential equations and eAte^{At}eAt

The system of equations below describes how the values of variables u1u_1u1​ and u2u_2u2​ affect each other over time:

Differential equations dudt=Au\frac{du}{dt} = Audtdu​=Au

By looking at the equations above, we might guess that over time u1u_1u1​ will decrease. We can get the same sort of information more safely by looking at the eigenvalues of the matrix A=[−121−2]A = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ 1 & -2 \end{bmatrix}A=[−11​2−2​] of our system dudt=Au\frac{du}{dt} = Audtdu​=Au. Stability

Not all systems have a steady state. The eigenvalues of AAA will tell us what sort of solutions to expect:

  1. Stability: u(t)→0u(t) \to 0u(t)→0 when Re(λ)<0Re(\lambda) < 0Re(λ)<0.
  2. Steady state: One eigenvalue is 000 and all other eigenvalues have negative real part.
  3. Blow up: if Re(λ)>0Re(\lambda) > 0Re(λ)>0 for any eigenvalue λ\lambdaλ.

If a two by two matrix A=[abcd]A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}A=[ac​bd​] has two eigenvalues with negative real part, its trace a+da+da+d is negative. The converse is not true: [−2001]\begin{bmatrix} -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}[−20​01​] has negative trace but one of its eigenvalues is 111 and e1te^{1t}e1t blows up. If AAA has a positive determinant and negative trace then the corresponding solutions must be stable.

Second order

We can change the second order equation y′′+by′+ky=0y'' + by' + ky = 0y′′+by′+ky=0 into a two by two first order system using a method similar to the one we used to find a formula for the Fibonacci numbers. If u=[y′y]u = \begin{bmatrix} y' \\ y \end{bmatrix}u=[y′y​], then

u′=[y′′y′]=[−b−k10][y′y].u' = \begin{bmatrix} y'' \\ y' \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -b & -k \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} y' \\ y \end{bmatrix}.u′=[y′′y′​]=[−b1​−k0​][y′y​].

We could use the methods we just learned to solve this system, and that would give us a solution to the second order scalar equation we started with.

If we start with a kkkth order equation we get a kkk by kkk matrix with coefficients of the equation in the first row and 111's on a diagonal below that; the rest of the entries are 000.
