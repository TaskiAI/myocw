Session Overview

We apply the method of elimination to all matrices, invertible or not. Counting the pivots gives us the rank of the matrix. Further simplifying the matrix puts it in reduced row echelon form R and improves our description of the null space.

Solving Ax=0Ax = 0Ax=0: pivot variables, special solutions

We have a definition for the column space and the nullspace of a matrix, but how do we compute these subspaces?

Computing the nullspace

The nullspace of a matrix AAA is made up of the vectors xxx for which Ax=0Ax = 0Ax=0. Suppose:

A=[1222246836810]A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 & 6 & 8 \\ 3 & 6 & 8 & 10 \end{bmatrix}A=​123​246​268​2810​​

(Note that the columns of this matrix AAA are not independent.) Our algorithm for computing the nullspace of this matrix uses the method of elimination, despite the fact that AAA is not invertible. We don't need to use an augmented matrix because the right side (the vector bbb) is 000 in this computation.

The row operations used in the method of elimination don't change the solution to Ax=bAx = bAx=b so they don't change the nullspace. (They do affect the column space.)

The first step of elimination gives us:

[1222246836810]→[122200240024]\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 & 6 & 8 \\ 3 & 6 & 8 & 10 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 \end{bmatrix}​123​246​268​2810​​→​100​200​222​244​​

We don't find a pivot in the second column, so our next pivot is the 222 in the third column of the second row:

[122200240024]→[122200240000]=U\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = U​100​200​222​244​​→​100​200​220​240​​=U

The matrix UUU is in echelon (staircase) form. The third row is zero because row 3 was a linear combination of rows 1 and 2; it was eliminated.

The rank of a matrix AAA equals the number of pivots it has. In this example, the rank of AAA (and of UUU) is 222.

Special solutions

Once we've found UUU we can use back-substitution to find the solutions xxx to the equation Ux=0Ux = 0Ux=0. In our example, columns 1 and 3 are pivot columns containing pivots, and columns 2 and 4 are free columns. We can assign any value to x2x_2x2​ and x4x_4x4​; we call these free variables. Suppose x2=1x_2 = 1x2​=1 and x4=0x_4 = 0x4​=0. Then:

2x3+4x4=0  ⟹  x3=02x_3 + 4x_4 = 0 \implies x_3 = 02x3​+4x4​=0⟹x3​=0

and:

x1+2x2+2x3+2x4=0  ⟹  x1=−2x_1 + 2x_2 + 2x_3 + 2x_4 = 0 \implies x_1 = -2x1​+2x2​+2x3​+2x4​=0⟹x1​=−2

So one solution is x=[−2100]x = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}x=​−2100​​ (because the second column is just twice the first column). Any multiple of this vector is in the nullspace.

Letting a different free variable equal 1 and setting the other free variables equal to zero gives us other vectors in the nullspace. For example:

x=[20−21]x = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}x=​20−21​​

has x4=1x_4 = 1x4​=1 and x2=0x_2 = 0x2​=0. The nullspace of AAA is the collection of all linear combinations of these "special solution" vectors.

The rank rrr of AAA equals the number of pivot columns, so the number of free columns is n−rn - rn−r: the number of columns (variables) minus the number of pivot columns. This equals the number of special solution vectors and the dimension of the nullspace.

Reduced row echelon form

By continuing to use the method of elimination we can convert UUU to a matrix RRR in reduced row echelon form (rref form), with pivots equal to 111 and zeros above and below the pivots.

U=[122200240000]→[120−200240000]→[120−200120000]=RU = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 0 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = RU=​100​200​220​240​​→​100​200​020​−240​​→​100​200​010​−220​​=R

By exchanging some columns, RRR can be rewritten with a copy of the identity matrix in the upper left corner, possibly followed by some free columns on the right. If some rows of AAA are linearly dependent, the lower rows of the matrix RRR will be filled with zeros:

R=[IF00]R = \begin{bmatrix} I & F \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}R=[I0​F0​]

(Here III is an rrr by rrr square matrix.)

If NNN is the nullspace matrix N=[−FI]N = \begin{bmatrix} -F \\ I \end{bmatrix}N=[−FI​] then RN=0RN = 0RN=0. (Here III is an n−rn - rn−r by n−rn - rn−r square matrix and 000 is an mmm by n−rn - rn−r matrix.) The columns of NNN are the special solutions.

Problem 7.1:

a) Find the row reduced form of:

A=[157904172−211−3]A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 7 & 9 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & 7 \\ 2 & -2 & 11 & -3 \end{bmatrix}A=​102​54−2​7111​97−3​​

b) What is the rank of this matrix?

c) Find any special solutions to the equation Ax=0Ax = 0Ax=0.

Solution:

a) To transform A into its reduced row form, we perform a series of row operations. Different operations are possible (same answer!). First, we multiply the first row by 2 and subtract it from the third row:

[157904172−211−3]→[157904170−12−3−21]\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 7 & 9 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & 7 \\ 2 & -2 & 11 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 7 & 9 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & 7 \\ 0 & -12 & -3 & -21 \end{bmatrix}​102​54−2​7111​97−3​​→​100​54−12​71−3​97−21​​

We then multiply the second row by 14\frac{1}{4}41​ to make the second pivot 1:

[157904170−12−3−21]→[1579011/47/40−12−3−21]\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 7 & 9 \\ 0 & 4 & 1 & 7 \\ 0 & -12 & -3 & -21 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 7 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & 1/4 & 7/4 \\ 0 & -12 & -3 & -21 \end{bmatrix}​100​54−12​71−3​97−21​​→​100​51−12​71/4−3​97/4−21​​

Multiply the second row by 12 and add it to the third row:

[1579011/47/40−12−3−21]→[1579011/47/40000]\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 7 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & 1/4 & 7/4 \\ 0 & -12 & -3 & -21 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 7 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & 1/4 & 7/4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}​100​51−12​71/4−3​97/4−21​​→​100​510​71/40​97/40​​

Finally, multiply the second row by 5 and subtract it from the first row:

[1579011/47/40000]→[1023/41/4011/47/40000]\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 7 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & 1/4 & 7/4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 23/4 & 1/4 \\ 0 & 1 & 1/4 & 7/4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}​100​510​71/40​97/40​​→​100​010​23/41/40​1/47/40​​

b) The matrix is of rank 2 because it has 2 pivots.

c) The special solutions to Ax=0Ax = 0Ax=0 are:

[−23/4−1/410]and[−1/4−7/401]\begin{bmatrix} -23/4 \\ -1/4 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{and} \quad \begin{bmatrix} -1/4 \\ -7/4 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}​−23/4−1/410​​and​−1/4−7/401​​
