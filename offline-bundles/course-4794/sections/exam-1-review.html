Exam 1 review

This lecture is a review for the exam. The majority of the exam is on what we've learned about rectangular matrices.

Sample question 1

Suppose u,vu, vu,v and www are non-zero vectors in R7R^7R7. They span a subspace of R7R^7R7. What are the possible dimensions of that vector space?

The answer is 1, 2 or 3. The dimension can't be higher because a basis for this subspace has at most three vectors. It can't be 0 because the vectors are non-zero.

Short questions

There may not be true/false questions on the exam, but it's a good idea to review these:

  1. Given a square matrix AAA whose nullspace is just {0}\{0\}{0}, what is the nullspace of ATA^TAT?

N(AT)N(A^T)N(AT) is also {0}\{0\}{0} because AAA is square.

  1. Do the invertible matrices form a subspace of the vector space of 5 by 5 matrices?

No. The sum of two invertible matrices may not be invertible. Also, 0 is not invertible, so is not in the collection of invertible matrices.

  1. True or false: If B2=0B^2 = 0B2=0, then it must be true that B=0B = 0B=0.

False. We could have B=[0100]B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}B=[00​10​].

  1. True or false: A system Ax=bAx = bAx=b of nnn equations with nnn unknowns is solvable for every right hand side bbb if the columns of AAA are independent.

True. AAA is invertible, and x=A−1bx = A^{-1}bx=A−1b is a (unique) solution.

  1. True or false: If m=nm = nm=n then the row space equals the column space.

False. The dimensions are equal, but the spaces are not. A good example to look at is B=[1000]B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}B=[10​00​].

  1. True or false: The matrices AAA and −A-A−A share the same four spaces.

True, because whenever a vector vvv is in a space, so is −v-v−v.

  1. True or false: If AAA and BBB have the same four subspaces, then AAA is a multiple of BBB.

A good way to approach this question is to first try to convince yourself that it isn't true – look for a counterexample. If AAA is 3 by 3 and invertible, then its row and column space are both R3R^3R3 and its nullspaces are {0}\{0\}{0}. If BBB is any other invertible 3 by 3 matrix it will have the same four subspaces, and it may not be a multiple of AAA. So we answer “false”. It's good to ask how we could truthfully complete the statement “If AAA and BBB have the same four subspaces, then ...”

  1. If we exchange two rows of AAA, which subspaces stay the same?

The row space and the nullspace stay the same.

  1. Why can't a vector v=[123]v = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}v=​123​​ be in the nullspace of AAA and also be a row of AAA?

Because if vvv is the nthn^{th}nth row of AAA, the nthn^{th}nth component of the vector AvAvAv would be 14, not 0. The vector vvv could not be a solution to Av=0Av = 0Av=0. In fact, we will learn that the row space is perpendicular to the nullspace.
