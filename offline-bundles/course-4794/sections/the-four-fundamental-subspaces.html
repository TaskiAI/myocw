Lecture Notes

The four fundamental subspaces

In this lecture we discuss the four fundamental spaces associated with a matrix and the relations between them.

Four subspaces

Any mmm by nnn matrix AAA determines four subspaces (possibly containing only the zero vector):

Column space, C(A)C(A)C(A)

C(A)C(A)C(A) consists of all combinations of the columns of AAA and is a vector space in Rm\mathbb{R}^mRm.

Nullspace, N(A)N(A)N(A)

This consists of all solutions xxx of the equation Ax=0Ax = 0Ax=0 and lies in Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn.

Row space, C(AT)C(A^T)C(AT)

The combinations of the row vectors of AAA form a subspace of Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn. We equate this with C(AT)C(A^T)C(AT), the column space of the transpose of AAA.

Left nullspace, N(AT)N(A^T)N(AT)

We call the nullspace of ATA^TAT the left nullspace of AAA. This is a subspace of Rm\mathbb{R}^mRm.

Basis and Dimension

Column space

The rrr pivot columns form a basis for C(A)C(A)C(A)

dim C(A)=r.\text{dim } C(A) = r.dim C(A)=r.

Nullspace

The special solutions to Ax=0Ax = 0Ax=0 correspond to free variables and form a basis for N(A)N(A)N(A). An mmm by nnn matrix has n−rn - rn−r free variables:

dim N(A)=n−r.\text{dim } N(A) = n - r.dim N(A)=n−r.

Row space

We could perform row reduction on ATA^TAT, but instead we make use of RRR, the row reduced echelon form of AAA.

Although the column spaces of AAA and RRR are different, the row space of RRR is the same as the row space of AAA. The rows of RRR are combinations of the rows of AAA, and because reduction is reversible the rows of AAA are combinations of the rows of RRR. The first rrr rows of RRR are the “echelon” basis for the row space of AAA:

dim C(AT)=r.\text{dim } C(A^T) = r.dim C(AT)=r.

Left nullspace

The matrix ATA^TAT has mmm columns. We just saw that rrr is the rank of ATA^TAT, so the number of free columns of ATA^TAT must be m−rm - rm−r:

dim N(AT)=m−r.\text{dim } N(A^T) = m - r.dim N(AT)=m−r.

The left nullspace is the collection of vectors yyy for which ATy=0A^T y = 0ATy=0. Equivalently, yTA=0y^T A = 0yTA=0; here yyy and 000 are row vectors. We say “left nullspace” because yTy^TyT is on the left of AAA in this equation. To find a basis for the left nullspace we reduce an augmented version of AAA:

[Am×nIm×m]⟶[Rm×nEm×m].\begin{bmatrix} A_{m \times n} & I_{m \times m} \end{bmatrix} \longrightarrow \begin{bmatrix} R_{m \times n} & E_{m \times m} \end{bmatrix}.[Am×n​​Im×m​​]⟶[Rm×n​​Em×m​​].

From this we get the matrix EEE for which EA=REA = REA=R. (If AAA is a square, invertible matrix then E=A−1E = A^{-1}E=A−1.) In our example,

EA=[−1201−10−101][123111211231]=[101101100000]=R.EA = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & -1 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = R.EA=​−11−1​2−10​001​​​111​212​323​111​​=​100​010​110​100​​=R.

The bottom m−rm - rm−r rows of EEE describe linear dependencies of rows of AAA, because the bottom m−rm - rm−r rows of RRR are zero. Here m−r=1m - r = 1m−r=1 (one zero row in RRR). The bottom m−rm - rm−r rows of EEE satisfy the equation yTA=0y^T A = 0yTA=0 and form a basis for the left nullspace of AAA.

New vector space

The collection of all 3×33 \times 33×3 matrices forms a vector space; call it MMM. We can add matrices and multiply them by scalars and there's a zero matrix (additive identity). If we ignore the fact that we can multiply matrices by each other, they behave just like vectors. Some subspaces of MMM include:

  • all upper triangular matrices
  • all symmetric matrices
  • DDD, all diagonal matrices

DDD is the intersection of the first two spaces. Its dimension is 333; one basis for DDD is:

Exercises on the four fundamental subspaces

Problem 10.1:

(3.6 #11. Introduction to Linear Algebra: Strang) A is an mmm by nnn matrix of rank rrr. Suppose there are right sides bbb for which Ax=bAx = bAx=b has no solution.

a) What are all the inequalities (<<< or ≤\le≤) that must be true between mmm, nnn, and rrr?

b) How do you know that ATy=0A^T y = 0ATy=0 has solutions other than y=0y = 0y=0?

Solution:

a) The rank of a matrix is always less than or equal to the number of rows and columns, so r≤mr \le mr≤m and r≤nr \le nr≤n. The second statement tells us that the column space is not all of Rm\mathbb{R}^mRm, so r<mr < mr<m.

b) These solutions make up the left nullspace, which has dimension m−r>0m - r > 0m−r>0 (that is, there are nonzero vectors in it).

Problem 10.2:

(3.6 #24.) ATy=dA^T y = dATy=d is solvable when ddd is in which of the four subspaces? The solution yyy is unique when the _ contains only the zero vector.

Solution:

It is solvable when ddd is in the row space, which consists of all vectors ATyA^T yATy. The solution yyy is unique when the left nullspace contains only the zero vector.
